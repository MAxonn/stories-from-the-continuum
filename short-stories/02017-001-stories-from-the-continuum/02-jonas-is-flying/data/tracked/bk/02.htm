The elevator’s doors slam shut with a soft thump leaving its occupants in complete darkness. There’s barely enough space for the two men to huddle together, crouching, with the back of their necks pressed firmly against the metal ceiling. They wait for the elevator to start moving. Nothing happens. Within seconds, the metal box turns into an inspiration for their worst nightmares. The silence is suffocating. Cold sweat. Racing hearts. Frozen thought[A1] .

With a subtle jolt, their prison starts its descent. One of the men lets out a sigh of relief. He shuffles, trying to shift his[A2]  weight from his aching knees. His partner is tense like a bow’s string and unmoving like a rock. The smell of sewage they felt when entering the building is getting worse. It doesn’t help them to think that the elevator’s destination is about thirty meters below street level.

“I sure hope we don’t splash into anything”, says a trembling, weary voice.

“Shut up old man”, comes the stiff answer. They don’t know each other by name, having met about an hour ago on a nearby street.
